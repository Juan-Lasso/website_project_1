Fewer kids, bigger bills: Is Valley Stream 13 per-student spending sustainable?

Are communities shouldering higher costs for fewer students and increased spending?

By Juan Lasso | November 18, 2024

Valley Stream District 13 has seen a thinning student population for years, with over 220 fewer students enrolled today than a decade ago. Yet, as student enrollment has fallen, the amount of money spent on their learning continues to tick up. Adjusted for inflation, the district spent $20,457 per student in 2014. Flash forward to today, that figure has climbed to $25,314.

District 13's per-student spending is not a statistically striking sum compared to the state average — $26,000 — but nationally, it ranks on the high end. Policymakers have questioned the sustainability of an ever-soaring school funding standard, especially for seemingly overfunded districts with declining enrollment.

“Schools have pretty fixed costs,” said David Bloomfield, an education professor at Brooklyn College and the CUNY Graduate Center. But when those fixed expenses are spread over a dwindling student population, it “puts financial pressure on the district locally and in terms of state funding.”
